01 · The Problem

Concept Without a Visual System

KHEM possessed a strong core concept—traditional Pharaonic heritage merged with modern Alexandrian streetwear. However, the brand materials were scattered, disjointed, and failed to tell a unified story.

The challenge was to represent ancient heritage in a modern premium style—avoiding tourist souvenir aesthetics or generic calligraphy templates.

02 · Strategy

The Archetype Before Design

Before starting visual design, I defined the brand archetype: The Ruler with a secondary touch of The Creator. Confident, authoritative, and creative—crafting high-quality garments that project power and heritage.

The tone of voice was set to confident, timeless, and Egyptian. The color palette was curated from historical references: Deep Obsidian Black, Pharaonic Gold, Rich Crimson Red, and Cream Sand.

From Disjointed Concept To Unified System
